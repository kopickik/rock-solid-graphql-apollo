Lebron's Life

Lebron James has led a very interesting life. He was born on December 30th 1984, in Akron, Ohio. At an early age, Lebron showed his outstanding talent.


As a freshman in high school, he scored an average of eighteen points per game. In July 1999, with friends Sian Cotton, Dru Joyce and Willie McGee, James, at age 14, helped his Akron AAU team to a national runner-up finish in Orlando, Fla. In March, 2000, as a 15-year-old freshman, James scored 25 points and grabbed nine rebounds to lead St. Vincent-St. Mary High School to a state championship victory over Jamestown Greenview.

Later, in Feb., 2002, James was pictured on Sports Illustrated magazine cover with the title, “The Chosen One.”

  • March 20, 2002: Named Ohio’s Mr. Basketball a second time.
  • Feb. 8, 2003: Coming back after a suspension for accepting free jerseys, scores 52 points in win over Westchester (Calif.) High School.
  • March 17, 2003: Again named Ohio’s Mr. Basketball, becoming first player to win the award three times.
  • March 17, 2003: Again named Ohio’s Mr. Basketball, becoming first player to win the award three times.
  • March 22, 2003: Scores 25 points and has 11 rebounds to lead the Irish to mythical national championship and a third state title by beating Kettering Alter.
  • May 21, 2003: Receives a $90 million endorsement contract with Nike.
  • May 22, 2003: Cavaliers win NBA Draft lottery in Secaucus, N.J., ensuring James will play for his hometown pro team

Little Known Facts

Some things that are interesting about Lebron James that not many people know about. Lebron James was the first one picked in the overall draft in 2003. With thirteen years to look back on, that year's class ranks among the most successful in the league's history. Lebron James not only loves basketball, he acutally also played football in high school. He also runs a non-profit organization called Family Foundation, which helps needy children in his hometown area. Lebron James has been named the NBA's Most Valuable Player four times (2009, 2010, 2012, 2013). He has been to the NBA All-Star Game every year since 2005. He owned a very small stake in Beats Electronics, which was sold to Apple, Inc. for $3 billion in June 2014, reportedly netting him around $30 million in cash and stocks [3]. Lebron has also played for the US national team in the 2004, 2008, and 2012 Olympics. They won gold in their last two appearances.
